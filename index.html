My Culture

Vietnamese culture is rich in history, traditions, and strong family values. Respect for elders is very important, and many families spend time together during special celebrations such as Tet, the Lunar New Year. Traditional foods like phở, bánh mi, and cơm tấm are also an important part of Vietnamese culture.

1.Important parts of Vietnamese culture

Vietnamese culture is rich in history and traditions, with strong family values, deep respect for elders and ancestors, and a strong sense of community; traditional foods such as Phở,Bánh Mì, and Cơm Tấm are an important part of everyday life, while major celebrations like Tết bring families together to celebrate, share special meals, visit relatives, and honor their ancestors, and traditional clothing such as the Áo dài represents the beauty and elegance of Vietnamese heritage, while customs like respecting teachers, valuing education, practicing traditional arts and music, and participating in community festivals help preserve the culture and pass it on to future generations.

2. Vietnam top 5 famous food.

1. Pho

Phở or pho is a Vietnamese soup dish consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat – usually beef, and sometimes chicken. Phở is a popular food in Vietnam where it is served in households, street-stalls, and restaurants nationwide.

2. Com Tam

Cơm tấm, also referred as cơm sườn, is a Vietnamese dish made from broken rice, ribs, cucumbers, and pickled carrots, often served with sweet and sour fish sauce. Tấm refers to the broken rice grains, while cơm refers to cooked rice.

3. Banh Mi

In Vietnamese cuisine, bánh mì, bánh mỳ or banh mi, is a short baguette with thin, crisp crust and a soft, airy texture. It is often split lengthwise and filled with meat and savory ingredients like a submarine sandwich and served as a meal, called bánh mì thịt. Plain bánh mì is also eaten as a staple food.

4.Bun Bo Hue

Bún bò Huế or bún bò is a Vietnamese rice noodle dish with sliced beef, chả lụa, and sometimes pork knuckles. The dish originates from Huế, a city in central Vietnam associated with the cooking style of the former royal court. The dish has a mix of spicy, salty, and savory flavors.

2.Vietnamese History

Vietnam has a long and proud history of defending its independence. Throughout history, the Vietnamese people fought against many powerful countries to protect their land, culture, and freedom. These victories are an important part of Vietnam’s national identity and show the strength and determination of the Vietnamese people.

Countries Vietnam fought in history

  • Chinese dynasties – many wars, independence gained in 938 AD
  • Mongol Empire – defeated in 1258, 1285, and 1288
  • France – defeated in 1954 (Battle of Dien Bien Phu)
  • Desperation": Starving Vietnamese being beaten by Japanese soldiers, the Vietnamese Famine 1945
  • In 1945, Vietnam transitioned from brutal Japanese occupation to independence as Japan surrendered
  • The Vietnam War ended on April 30, 1975, when North Vietnam captured Saigon, defeating South Vietnam and unifying the country under Communist rule.

4.Vietnam Top 5 Landmarks you shouldn't miss when you visit to VietNam

1.Ha Long Bay- Quang Ninh

Ha Long Bay in Vietnam is a famous tourist destination which is located on the West coast of the Gulf of Tonkin. With thousands of magnificent islands and many nearby attractions, the bay is a favorite destination of many tourists.

2.Hoi An Ancient Town

Hoi An Ancient Town is a remarkably preserved 15th-19th century trading port in central Vietnam, renowned for its yellow timber-frame shophouses, colorful lanterns, and rich cultural blend of Chinese, Japanese, and French influences. Located on the Thu Bon River, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site popular for tourism

3.Son Doong Cave- Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park

Hang Son Doong, located in Vietnam's Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park, is the world's largest natural cave. Formed 2-5 million years ago, it features massive passages (over 200m high), an internal jungle, a fast-flowing river, and 80-meter stalagmites. Giant sinkholes (dolines) allow sunlight to create a unique, self-contained ecosystem.

4.Golden Bridge- Bà Nà hills.

The Golden Hands Bridge is probably one of the most iconic landmarks in Vietnam! The bridge is slightly curved and supported gracefully by a pair of separate enormous hands. Walking the bridge also provides some of the most scenic and panoramic views of Da Nang city and the valley below.

5.Hue Imperial City

The Hue Imperial City, located in central Vietnam along the Perfume River, is a 19th-century walled fortress and palace complex that served as the capital of the Nguyen Dynasty, Vietnam's last ruling dynasty (1802–1945). Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it features stunning, traditional architecture that once housed royal palaces, temples, and gardens.
